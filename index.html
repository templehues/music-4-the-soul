Music that Feeds The Soul...

R&B and soul are two genres that I believe are the perfect way to uplift and show one's emotions. R&B, also known as Rhythm & Blues, has roots in musical genres like jazz, blues, gospel, boogie-woogie, and jump blues. R&B originated in the 1940s, dominated the charts, took over in the 1960s, and took the world by storm. Soul roots come from gospel music, and R&B originated in the late 1950s but only started dominating international charts in the 1960s. Hip-hop is both a musical genre and a culture among black people. Hip-hop originated in the 1970s by African Americans in Brox, New York; it was only in the 1980s that it was diversified, developed into more complex styles, and spread around the world.

These 3 genres have a greater impact on the black community and have helped black people voice out their struggles and emotions and help cope with them. Hip-hop is more education when it comes to learning about the political problems POC face in different parts of the world, but it also helps artists express problems and emotions they cannot talk about. Music is by far the best way to express your thoughts and every emotion you can think of, from extremely happy to depressed if you need a way to express yourself.
